Before the Month Hall Game, there will be a dot memory task, that you have just practiced.

In this game, Monty Hall, a thoroughly honest game-show host, has placed a car behind one of three doors.

There is a goat behind each of the other two doors.

First, Monty will ask you to pick a door. Then he will open one of the other two doors, that you did not pick, to reveal one of the goats

Then, Monty will ask you to make a final choice between the remaining doors. Which door do you think has the highest chance to win the car?

If you were in the real game, would you stick with your initial choice or switch to the other door?

You will win whatever is behind the door you finally select.

The aim is to win the car.

Remember:

  • The car and goats are randomly placed behind the doors.
  • The unbiased host, Monty, knows where the car and goats are.
  • Monty does not re-shuffle the car's location once you begin playing each game.
  • Monty never opens your inital choice or reveals the car.
  • Monty always reveals one of the goats.
  • Monty always offers switch/stick options to the guests.
